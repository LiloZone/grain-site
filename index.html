GRAIN

BOTANIC + PAPER + MAPPING

About

CONCEPT & THEME

GRAIN is an investigation inspired by 3 main concepts, the botanical garden (or parks) as a nature library, the sustainable architecture models and the symbiotic relations along plants. The research process will be transformed into an audiovisual installation, with a mapping projection over a mobile paper architecture structure, combining few other elements such as sand, concrete and leaves.

The Bothanical Garden of Pavol Josef Safarik University will be our main visual source. We will make registers, explores textures, drawings and some species in the garden to experiment manually and digitally to create a video animation.
Parallel to the visual research, an investigation on bio architecture will be done. Much of contemporary architecture seeks biological inspiration, whether in the forms or functions in fauna and/or flora. The aim is to create customized structures for an ecosystem. In this sense, observing nature is the best for learning about housing, construction and energy consumption. For example, how does a seed know all its algorithm for creating a tree and generate fruits?
A symbiotic affinity can be defined as a mutual beneficial relationship, and that is the one we are interested in create and investigate. How can we learn from nature to build cities with more symbiotic relations among man, plants, concrete and machines?
An urban environment has too many variables, but when a problem is scaled and fits all within our field of vision, it is easier to select, understand, interact and improve ideas, observing all aspects. In that way, we will work with paper models, not to reproduce a city structure, but to simplify a format of building, creating abstract configurations that will be mapped with the video mentioned above. That model is a projected mobile model, working as a pop-up green hypothetical city-bioma.

MAPS, REPRESENTATION AND MODELS: MAPPING AS A CREATIVE PROCESS AND THE RELATIOHSHIP BETWEEN SCALES

The human being created representation systems to navigate the world. Whether in the form of drawings, graphs, maps or models. When a problem is scaled and fits all within our field of vision, it is easier to understand, interact and improve ideas, observing all aspects. For this we created several technologies: calculators, telescopes, lasers, cameras, etc. But technological devices alone are nothing. So, to understand and work with technologies, we create languages, subtitles, libraries, internet, etc.

Scaling problems and concerns is how children (and adults) begin to apprehend the world. A model mapped then, is a physical object that represents the world in a playful way, it is a small augmented reality to the naked eye that opens a physicall space beyond digital. And, perhaps because it relates so many real and virtual layers, it is possible to look at the problem and imagine other futures combining technologies and re-creating languages.

Team

FASCINATED BY EXPERIMENTS

Lina Lopes

MANAUS, Amazonas, Brazil

Lina Lopes is an artist and designer in Digital Media, works with Video Mapping projects, interactivity and technical lighting. Degree in Film from the University of São Paulo and specialized in Lighting and Design. Currently, she is a researcher at the Pos Graduate Program at University Anhembi-Morumbi about Math and Design.

She has 10 years of experience working with Videomapping, having made several projections for exhibitions, events, international festivals and public facades. Among her international projects are interactive projections developed for facades and festivals in Barcelona, Madrid, Geneva and Frankfurt. In Brazil, she was the technical director of the FIESP facade, in Avenida Paulista (2013 and 2014). She made several immersive installations, such as La Casa Azul, a project made for the Frida Kahlo exhibition at the Tomie Ohtake Institute (2015) and Vesica Piscis, an installation with videomapping for a dance performance at the Museum of Contemporary Art (2016).

Her research covers the parametric design fields, projection mapping, prototyping, interactivity, wearables and internet of things. Her production in the field of new media permeates the concept of interactive installation, programming with Arduino, Processing, Raspberry Pi, prioritizing image manipulation projects in real time. In São Paulo, she is the founder of LILO.ZONE – a hub and prototype factory dedicated to the creative use of technology.
